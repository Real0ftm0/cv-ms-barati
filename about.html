About

My name is Mina …, and I hold a Bachelor’s and Master’s degree in Architecture. Throughout my academic journey, I have sought to establish a meaningful connection between architecture, cultural identity, and social needs.

My undergraduate thesis, Carpet Museum Inspired by Kerman Carpets, allowed me to explore architecture rooted in local art and cultural heritage, demonstrating how design can reinterpret traditional values in contemporary contexts. During my Master’s studies, my thesis focused on the Autism House with an Emphasis on Social Interaction, where I examined how architectural spaces can improve the quality of life for individuals with special needs.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Urban Planning, Environmental Design, Urban Design, Socially Responsive Architecture, and Sustainable Architecture. In particular, I am passionate about exploring how human-centered design can both preserve cultural identity and respond to contemporary social and environmental challenges.

My goal for pursuing a Ph.D. is to conduct advanced research in socially responsive and sustainable architecture, focusing on the design of spaces that promote spatial justice, social inclusion, and improved quality of life. I aspire to contribute to the international academic community and collaborate with leading scholars to develop innovative approaches to human-centered and sustainable design.
